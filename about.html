Who we are.

DPM Design and Project Management is a Sydney drafting practice. We prepare DA, CDC and Construction Certificate documentation for NSW commercial projects, and manage the lodgement on the Planning Portal end to end.

Our Approach
One scope · Done well

One scope. Done well.

DPM's scope ends at the Construction Certificate. No tender. No construction administration. Drafting and approvals — and only that — gives us the focus to do them well.

The narrow brief is deliberate. NSW approval pathways have their own logic: the documentation a council expects for a DA is not the same documentation a private certifier wants for a CC. Working at the seam between them — daily — produces faster turnaround and fewer RFIs.

How We Work
Three principles

Three principles. Every project.

01

Pre-empt the RFI.

The fastest approval is the one that doesn't get bounced back. Our documentation is built to anticipate the questions a council assessor or certifier will ask — and answer them up front. SEEs are written against the LEP and DCP clause-by-clause. Drawings are coordinated to the SEE. Compliance statements are built into the package, not bolted on after.

02

Evidence-based pathway.

DA or CDC isn't a coin flip. We assess against the relevant SEPP and LEP up front and recommend the pathway most likely to land — saving fees that get wasted on the wrong submission. If the project doesn't comply with CDC on a single line, we say so and pivot to DA. Confidence in the pathway means confidence in the timeline.

03

Fixed-fee, where possible.

For most commercial fitout scopes, we provide a fixed-fee proposal once the brief is reviewed. No hourly surprises. Council and certifier fees are passed through at cost. Two-business-day response on every brief, every time. Clarity on cost and timeline before any work begins.

§ Project Types
NSW commercial fitouts

Commercial fitouts. NSW only.

A deliberately narrow brief. The clearer the scope, the cleaner the deliverable. We work across hospitality, retail and small commercial fitouts in NSW.

01 — Hospitality

Café & Hospitality

Class 6 cafés, restaurants, takeaways and small-bar fitouts. Food premises against AS 4674, accessibility under AS 1428.1, and the operational provisions councils scrutinise most.

02 — Retail

Retail Tenancies

Boutique retail, specialty stores, shopping centre tenancies. Centre management coordination, signage code compliance, and the mall-specific delivery requirements that complicate lodgement.

03 — Commercial

Commercial & Mixed-Use

Office tenancies, professional suites, mixed retail-and-residential ground floors. Class 5 and Class 6 fitouts where occupancy, fire and accessibility need careful resolution.

Out of Scope
Three boundaries

What we don't do.

A clear brief has edges. Three things sit outside the scope of the practice — and they're not on the roadmap.

×

Tender & Builder Selection

We don't run tender packages or recommend builders. Once approval is secured, we hand off to your chosen contractor without holding a financial interest.

×

Construction Administration

No site supervision, progress claims, or variation negotiation. The CC is our endpoint. Once construction begins, we're available for clarifications but not on-site delivery.

×

Residential New Builds

No new dwellings, duplexes, or major residential alterations. The practice is built around the rhythm of commercial approvals — not residential ones.
