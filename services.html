Our Services

Six services covering every NSW approval.

From drafting alone through to a complete approval package — DA, CDC, CC, Planning Portal lodgement and BCA compliance. Engaged à la carte or as a full package.

01
Architectural Drafting

Drawings drafted to clear approval.

Drafting is the foundation. Even the strongest concept design fails at council if the drawings can't be assessed against statutory provisions. We translate your concept into a council-ready drawing set — coordinated, dimensioned, and built to pre-empt the assessor's questions before they're asked.

How we assist

Whether you're starting with a sketch, a hand-drawn plan, or another consultant's documentation, we develop the drawings to a standard that satisfies councils, certifiers and the NSW Planning Portal — with accessibility, fire and egress, and food premises provisions resolved at the drafting stage rather than at lodgement.

What we deliver

  • Floor plans, sections, elevations and reflected ceiling plans
  • Joinery elevations and key construction details
  • Finishes, fixtures and equipment schedules
  • Site analysis and existing tenancy survey drawings
  • Coordinated PDF drawing set issued to council standard
Typical turnaround: 2 weeks for a standard fitout drawing set, depending on scope and consultant input.
02
Development Applications

The full DA pathway, managed.

A Development Application is the most flexible NSW approval pathway, but also the slowest. It's the right route when the project requires merit assessment — change of use, signage outside exempt thresholds, fitouts that exceed CDC limits, or any project where the council needs to weigh competing interests.

The DA process

  1. Site analysis & classification — building class under the NCC, occupancy, and statutory provisions identified.
  2. Pre-DA discussions with council where the project is sensitive enough to warrant a meeting before lodgement.
  3. Drawing set preparation — site, plans, sections, elevations, signage, and proposed/existing comparisons.
  4. Statement of Environmental Effects (SEE) — written against the relevant LEP and DCP, addressing every consideration the council will assess.
  5. Supporting reports coordinated — acoustic, traffic, hydraulic, waste management, where required.
  6. Planning Portal lodgement — case created, fees paid, owner's consent attached.
  7. RFI response — managed through to determination.

What we deliver

  • Full DA-ready drawing set
  • Statement of Environmental Effects (SEE)
  • Plan of Management where required
  • Signage schedule and street elevations
  • Coordination with consultants and council pre-lodgement
  • Planning Portal lodgement and RFI response
Council assessment: 6–12 weeks typical, dependent on the council, scale, and any RFI rounds. Documentation prep: 3–4 weeks.
03
Complying Development

The fast-track approval pathway.

A Complying Development Certificate is approval by checklist. If the project meets every clause of the relevant State Environmental Planning Policy (SEPP), a private certifier — not council — can issue the certificate, often within 20 business days. The trade-off: zero room for merit assessment. Comply or don't.

The CDC process

  1. Compliance assessment against the relevant SEPP, clause by clause. If the project doesn't comply on a single line, CDC isn't the right pathway.
  2. Pathway recommendation — we say so on evidence, not optimism. If CDC fails, we pivot to DA before fees are wasted.
  3. Drawing set preparation to CDC standard, with every clause cross-referenced.
  4. Compliance statements drafted clause-by-clause to pre-empt the certifier's checklist.
  5. Certifier engagement — appointed certifier briefed and engaged.
  6. Lodgement & certificate issued — CDC granted, construction can commence.

What we deliver

  • Code compliance assessment against the relevant SEPP
  • Floor plans, sections, elevations to CDC standard
  • Compliance statements clause-by-clause
  • Coordination with the appointed certifier
  • Planning Portal lodgement
Certifier turnaround: 20 business days statutory maximum. Documentation prep: 2–3 weeks.
04
Construction Certificates

From DA approval to building permit.

A DA approval is a planning consent — it doesn't allow construction to start. The Construction Certificate is the document that does. The drawings step up in detail, the conditions of consent get resolved, BCA compliance gets confirmed, and a certifier issues the CC. Only then does the builder pick up tools.

The CC process

  1. Conditions of consent review — every condition imposed by council mapped to a drawing or document response.
  2. Detailed construction drawings — dimensions, setouts, joinery details, finishes, every line a builder needs.
  3. BCA compliance statements against NCC 2022, including fire and egress, accessibility, and sanitary provisions.
  4. Consultant coordination — structural, hydraulic, electrical and mechanical drawings integrated.
  5. Certifier issues CC — construction can commence.

If the DA was lodged by another party, we can pick up the project at CC stage and produce the documentation set forward from the approved drawings.

What we deliver

  • Conditions of consent matrix
  • Issue-for-construction drawing set
  • BCA compliance statements
  • Coordination with structural, hydraulic, electrical consultants
  • CC documentation issued to certifier
Documentation: 2–4 weeks. Certifier issues CC: typically within 2–4 weeks of complete documentation.
05
Planning Portal Lodgement

The NSW Planning Portal, handled.

Since 1 July 2021, every DA, CDC and CC in NSW must be lodged through the NSW Planning Portal. The portal is its own quiet bottleneck — strict naming conventions, mandatory document types, owner's consent forms, statutory declarations, fee schedules. Get one upload wrong and the whole submission stalls.

How we assist

We manage the full Planning Portal workflow end to end — case creation, document preparation to the portal's naming standards, fee calculation, and RFI response. Engaged on its own for clients who have their own drawings but want the lodgement handled cleanly, or rolled into any of our documentation services.

What we deliver

  • NSW Planning Portal case creation and management
  • Document preparation to portal naming conventions
  • Owner's consent, cost estimate and statutory declarations
  • Council and certifier liaison
  • RFI response and re-submission management
Lodgement: 2–3 business days from receipt of complete documentation.
06
BCA / NCC Compliance

NCC 2022 compliance, independently assessed.

The Building Code of Australia (now under the National Construction Code 2022) is the regulatory backbone of every commercial fitout — fire and egress, accessibility, sanitary provisions, food premises. Standalone BCA reports are useful where a builder or designer has a drawing set and needs an independent compliance read before committing to the approval pathway.

How we assist

We produce comprehensive BCA / NCC compliance reports against current code, identifying every provision that applies to your project and confirming compliance — or, where Deemed-to-Satisfy isn't feasible, scoping a Performance Solution.

What we deliver

  • Building classification and characteristics assessment
  • Fire safety, egress and travel distance analysis
  • Accessibility under AS 1428.1
  • Sanitary provisions to NCC and Council requirements
  • Food premises against AS 4674 — 2004 (where applicable)
  • Performance Solutions scoping where required
Report delivery: 1–2 weeks for a standard fitout assessment.
Common Questions

Things clients often ask.

Quick answers to the questions that come up most. For anything specific to your project, send a brief.

How do I know if my project needs a DA or a CDC?

The CDC pathway is governed by State Environmental Planning Policies (SEPPs), which list every condition a project must meet to qualify. If your project complies with every clause of the relevant SEPP — including building height, setbacks, signage, and use type — CDC is faster and cheaper. If even one clause fails, the project must go through DA.

We assess this up front, on evidence, before recommending a pathway. The wrong choice wastes weeks and fees.

What's the difference between a DA approval and a Construction Certificate?

A DA approval is a planning consent — the council saying yes, this project is appropriate for this site. It does not authorise construction.

A Construction Certificate (CC) is the document a private certifier issues confirming the construction documentation complies with the BCA and the conditions of the DA consent. Construction can only legally commence once the CC is issued.

How long does a DA take to be approved?

Timeframes vary by council and complexity, but a typical commercial fitout DA in NSW is determined within 6 to 12 weeks of lodgement. Larger projects, projects requiring referral to external authorities (RMS, NSW Health, etc.), or projects with one or more RFI rounds can take longer.

Documentation prep adds another 3 to 4 weeks before lodgement.

Do you lodge directly on the NSW Planning Portal?

Yes. We act as the lodging party on the Planning Portal — creating the case, uploading documents to portal naming standards, attaching statutory declarations and owner's consent, paying fees, and managing all RFI correspondence through to determination.

Can you pick up a project mid-way through?

Yes. We regularly come in at CC stage when a DA was lodged by another consultant, or take over stalled DAs where the original drafter is no longer engaged. Send through what you have and we'll assess where to pick up.

Do you provide fixed-fee proposals?

For most commercial fitout scopes, yes. Once we've reviewed the brief and confirmed the pathway (DA / CDC / CC), we provide a fixed-fee proposal for the documentation phase. Council and certifier fees are passed through at cost. RFI response or scope changes are quoted separately if they fall outside the original brief.

Do you work outside NSW?

No. The practice is built around the specific rhythm of NSW councils, the NSW Planning Portal, and the SEPPs that govern complying development in this state. The expertise doesn't transfer cleanly to other jurisdictions, so we don't take on work outside NSW.
