Reference
Approval pathways

Common approval pathways.

A short reference to the pathways most often referenced in our work. The right route depends on the project, site and council — we recommend the most suitable option once the brief is reviewed.

DA

Development Application

Formal planning approval from council — used for change of use, new works, alterations and signage.

CDC

Complying Development Certificate

Faster, certifier-issued approval available when a project meets the conditions of the relevant SEPP.

CC

Construction Certificate

Issued after DA approval — confirms documentation complies with the BCA and conditions of consent. Authorises construction to commence.

OC

Occupation Certificate

Issued at the end of construction — confirms the building is suitable for occupation.

Common Questions
FAQ

Things clients often ask.

How do I know if my project needs a DA or a CDC?

If your project meets the conditions of the relevant State Environmental Planning Policy (SEPP), the CDC pathway is generally faster. If merit assessment is needed, the DA pathway is appropriate. We check your planning certificate and liaise with certifiers to confirm before documentation starts.

What's the difference between a DA and a Construction Certificate?

A DA is planning consent — it confirms the project is appropriate for the site. A CC is issued by a certifier confirming the construction documentation complies with the BCA and conditions of consent. Construction can't start until the CC is issued.

Can you do the CC if someone else did the DA?

Yes. We regularly pick up projects at the CC stage where another consultant handled the DA. Send through the approval and conditions of consent and we'll take it from there.

Can I use Planning Portal Submissions without doing my drawings with you?

Yes. Planning Portal Submissions is a standalone service. If you have your own drawings prepared by another consultant or in-house, we can lodge the application through the portal without doing the documentation work ourselves.

What does the $400 portal lodgement fee cover?

It covers setting up the application, entering all details, uploading and categorising documents, completing the portal workflow, and coordinating with you. Council, certifier and statutory fees are separate and paid by you directly.

Do you provide fixed-fee proposals for design work?

For most well-defined scopes, yes. Once the brief is reviewed and the pathway is confirmed, we provide a fixed-fee proposal. Council and certifier fees are passed through at cost.

Do you work outside NSW?

Yes. We support clients across Australia with design documentation. The Planning Portal service is NSW-specific, but design and documentation services cover all states.
