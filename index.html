The Approval Pathway
Brief to building permit

How a project moves from brief to building permit.

Every project follows roughly the same route through the NSW approval system. Where DPM picks up depends on where you are.

01

Brief & Site Assessment

Tenancy survey, base build review, building classification under the NCC, and an initial pathway recommendation — DA or CDC.

StageWeek 1
02

Concept Drafting

Floor plans, RCPs, sections and elevations developed against the brief. Accessibility and statutory provisions resolved at concept stage.

StageWeek 1 – 2
03

Authority Strategy

Final pathway selection on evidence — DA or CDC. Pre-DA discussions with council where warranted, certifier engagement where not.

StageWeek 2
04

Documentation & Reports

Drawing set finalised, the SEE drafted (DA) or compliance statements prepared (CDC), supporting consultant reports coordinated.

StageWeek 2 – 3
05

Planning Portal Lodgement

NSW Planning Portal case created, documents uploaded to portal naming standards, fees paid. Council or certifier accepts the lodgement.

StageWeek 3
06

Approval & Construction Certificate

RFI response handled, conditions reviewed, CC documentation produced from the approved drawings. Construction can commence.

DeterminationCouncil 6–12 wk · CDC 20 days
