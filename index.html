The Approval Pathway
Brief to building permit

How a project moves from brief to building permit.

Most projects follow a similar route through the approval system. We can pick up the brief at any stage — early concept, mid-design, or once a Development Application (DA) has been approved.

01

Brief & Site Assessment

Initial review of the project — site context, building classification under the NCC, and the most appropriate approval pathway: Development Application (DA) or Complying Development Certificate (CDC).

StageWeek 1
02

Concept & Design Documentation

Floor plans, sections and elevations developed against the brief. Accessibility, fire and statutory provisions considered at concept stage.

StageWeek 1 – 2
03

Approval Pathway

Pathway confirmed on evidence — DA, Complying Development Certificate (CDC) or another route. Pre-lodgement discussions with council or certifier as required.

StageWeek 2
04

Documentation & Reports

Drawing set finalised, Statement of Environmental Effects (SEE) drafted for DA or compliance statements prepared for CDC, with supporting consultant reports coordinated.

StageWeek 2 – 3
05

NSW Planning Portal Submission

Case created on the NSW Planning Portal, documents uploaded to the relevant naming standards, fees and owner's consent attached, submission accepted by council or certifier.

StageWeek 3
06

Approval

Requests for information managed, conditions of consent addressed, and Construction Certificate (CC) documentation prepared from the approved drawings so construction can commence.

DeterminationCouncil 6–12 wk · CDC 20 days
