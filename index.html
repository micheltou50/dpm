The Approval Pathway

From brief to building permit, in six stages.

Every project follows roughly the same route through the NSW approval system. Where DPM picks up depends on where you are.

1

Brief & Site Assessment

Tenancy survey, base build review, building classification under the NCC, and an initial pathway recommendation — DA or CDC. Outcomes documented before any drafting begins.

Week 1
2

Concept Drafting

Floor plans, RCPs, sections and elevations developed against the brief. Accessibility, FFE strategy and statutory provisions resolved at concept stage to avoid late-stage rework.

Week 1 – 2
3

Authority Strategy

Final pathway selection on evidence — DA, CDC, or a CDC where the SEPP fits. Pre-DA discussions with council where they're warranted, certifier engagement where they're not.

Week 2
4

Documentation & Reports

Drawing set finalised, the Statement of Environmental Effects drafted (DA) or compliance statements prepared (CDC), and supporting consultant reports coordinated where required.

Week 2 – 3
5

Planning Portal Lodgement

NSW Planning Portal case created, documents uploaded to portal naming standards, owner's consent and fee schedule attached. Council or certifier accepts the lodgement.

Week 3
6

Approval & Construction Certificate

RFI response handled, conditions of consent reviewed, and the Construction Certificate documentation produced from the approved drawings. CC issued; construction can commence.

Council 6–12 wk · CDC 20 days
