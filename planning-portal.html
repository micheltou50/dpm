02
Planning Portal Submissions

The NSW Planning Portal has its own logic — document naming conventions, owner's consent, statutory declarations, fee schedules and a workflow that trips people up if you haven't done it before. We handle the full lodgement process so the submission goes in clean and gets accepted first time. You don't need to have done your drawings with us — bring your own documentation and we'll lodge it.

What we lodge

Application types
DA · CDC · CC

Applications

Development Applications, Complying Development Certificates and Construction Certificates lodged through the portal with all applicant details, property information and documentation uploaded and categorised.

PCA

Principal Certifier Appointment

PCA appointment lodged on the portal for approved CC or CDC applications.

OC

Occupation Certificates

OC applications lodged through the portal once construction is complete.

Review

Documentation Review

Not sure if your documents are ready to lodge? We review what you've got, identify what's missing, and tell you what needs to be addressed before submission.

What's included in every lodgement

End to end

Setting up the application on the portal, entering all applicant and property details, uploading and categorising documents, completing the portal workflow, and coordinating with you on anything the portal needs.

Reference
Approval pathways

Common approval pathways.

A short reference to the pathways most often referenced in our work. The right route depends on the project, site and council — we recommend the most suitable option once the brief is reviewed.

DA

Development Application

Formal planning approval from council — used for change of use, new works, alterations and signage.

CDC

Complying Development Certificate

Faster, certifier-issued approval available when a project meets the conditions of the relevant SEPP.

CC

Construction Certificate

Issued after DA approval — confirms documentation complies with the BCA and conditions of consent. Authorises construction to commence.

OC

Occupation Certificate

Issued at the end of construction — confirms the building is suitable for occupation.
